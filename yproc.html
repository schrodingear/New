Procedure



  1. Fix the given wooden beam (meter scale) and the end of the meter scale is clamped to the table with the clamping arrangement.

  2. A small hole can be made to the beam at the other end so that weights can be fitted on the end.

  3. A telescope is focused on the pin.

  4. Weights are arranged on both sides of the beam.

  5. Observe the time for 30 oscillations of the beam using a stopwatch.

  6. For the same mass, repeat observation of time and find its mean and hence determine the period.

  7. The procedure is repeated for different masses, and corresponding periods of oscillation are determined.

  8. Plot the graph of T^2 versus M.
